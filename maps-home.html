MYNEN KORRENA
Reitelden

Reitelden

“On The City of Everbloom” by S.H. Yun, from the Arbor Keene public archives, 46,660NE:

To understand Reitelden is to understand the contemporary way of Mynen Korrena. Its location sets it right in the nautical center of 4 separate provinces--to the west, the Leistun Straight provides safe passage on calm seas to Mynakeyna and Syndulai; to the east, the River Rzeka provides routes to the entire western shore of Dawn. Reitelden goods make their way across every corner of the continent and beyond. Fresh fruits, lumber, and all manner of craftsmanship is exported from the city. Its economic power cannot be understated.

The city is well known for its contradictory nature: a port town built high above the sea. Its success can be attributed to its clever engineering: using a geyser found within the natural caves of the peninsula, the Civil Conjurers of Reitelden have harnessed a water-powered elevator, connecting hundreds of vessels to the aqueducts across the city.

To live in Reitelden is to take up a life of constant movement. In a great melting pot of cultures and religious worship, one thing seems to be universal in all the residents of this city: they work hard so they can play hard. Every worker is an artist, a devotee, a leader. Though it may not be the artistic capital of Mynen Korrena (that honor would go to Raton-Laveur), centuries of rich cultural history permeate the Mynen zeitgeist.

For 35 years, Archduchess Aldayah has overseen the city's machinations from her seat in the city's Cathedral Hall. When she isn't tending to her responsibilities on the Millevian Council, she can often be found leading spiritual sermons on the Kindlemother, Lithalaigh. Just like her subjects, she has a taste for the spectacle. Her political rivals claim her impassioned speeches aim more to awe her citizens more than inform and serve them; but none can deny, her bombastic displays of leadership set a precedent to work.

Your head spins when you walk these streets. So many sounds, colors, voices--it's too easy to get caught up in it all. One may expect the senses to dull in death, but you've long come to learn the opposite is true. Your vampiric nature has brought forth an acute awareness of your surroundings, an ability crucial in your deduction of secret knowledge. This sight is to your detriment here, however. It's like you're being flooded with information, every twist and turn in the tightly-packed city streets offering thousands and thousands of new points of interest at once.

You have to focus on the things that don't move in order not to feel dizzy. You observe the architecture: massive blocks of wood and stone, with sharp roofs of red-brown with walls of white and gold. Stunning plazas lined with perfectly-kept trees. Great arches and columns of marble. You bring your awareness to the city's foundation. You see the constant changes, the way the buildings are built and rebuilt and destroyed and renewed, all to the whims of the people. Like rings on a tree marking the years of its life, the very ground upon which you walk, the many places you visit made in different times, mark the decades of changing tastes.

Yet, you know this city is truly young. Its layers only go back a few millenia--maybe 30,000 years at best. Ancient to most, but you know that's only scratching this land's history. What lays beneath this stony foundation…?

You're shocked it's taken so long to make it to this city. Reiteldeniens seemed to always have the most to hide, always ending up the target of your watchful eye, and gods, the secrets you've seen! This place is a breeding ground for corruption and greed. From secret stashes of forbidden texts, to underground smuggling rings, to assassinations and plots, there's always something afoot here.

You can get whatever you need here. If you need supplies, manpower, or information, you know the best person to go to is Ronan. He runs a night club somewhere nearby the coliseum, and happens to be the biggest contraband smuggler in the Strait. It may be worth trying to cash in a favor if the need arises.

The enchanted lands of Reitelden! You've always been curious to see this city. Each summer, you found the simple joy of eating a Honey Cloudbloom after a difficult session of training, a fruit treasured for its muscle-soothing properties and sweet, tart taste.

You recall as a child, one of your personal guardsmen, Emil, telling you about his trips to Reitelden. He spoke fondly of the Tournament of Harvest, an annual celebration of the Cloudbloom trees sprouting their delicious fruit. Mighty warriors would take to the coliseum and fight a glorious battle, a gift of entertainment to the gods as thank you for their gracious gift of food. When witnessing this event, it was customary to wear elegant bright-colored outfits for the pre- and post-battle ceremonies--huge, elegant parties where all the city's high society can mingle. Emil would describe the battles with perfect drama and heroism, as the great swordsmen fought with graceful flourishes and elegant swings. The good guys always won, of course. You always pictured yourself in those fights.

You feel uneasiness walking these streets. The amount of love in this city is palpable, yes. You feel a burning passion, a raging fire of emotion in the souls of these people. But it's… empty. Unguided. Moving in too many directions at once. They build their worlds around their vices and turn their heads down from their ancestors.

The people here hold no memory of the Flood. Not in their minds, not in their blood.

By the year 380, the world has transformed. Fantastic developments in innovation, both arcane and mechanical, sweep the globe at an exhilerating speed. Flying ships, mass locomotives, firearms, all manner of awe-striking inventions. One nation, however, has elected to turn their shoulder on a changing world. They reject the technology upon which the New World is being built. In fact, they reject the use of magic altogether. And yet, they remain a titan of a nation, a terrifying force to be reckoned with. Welcome to Laradel.
